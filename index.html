Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common NRI tax queries and concerns

Do NRIs need to file tax returns in India?

+

Yes, NRIs are required to file ITR if their Indian income exceeds ‚Çπ2.5 lakhs or if they have any taxable income in India, including rental income, capital gains, or business income. Filing is also mandatory if TDS has been deducted from your Indian income.

Can NRIs avoid double taxation on their income?

+

Yes, NRIs can avoid double taxation by claiming benefits under DTAA (Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement) between India and their country of residence. You can claim tax credits or exemptions as per the treaty provisions.

What is the capital gains tax rate for NRIs?

+

For NRIs, short-term capital gains are taxed at 30% (plus surcharge and cess), while long-term capital gains on property are taxed at 20% with indexation benefits. Equity investments have different rates - consult our experts for detailed guidance.

How long does the tax filing process take?

+

Typically, we complete tax filings within 7-10 business days once all documents are received. For complex cases involving capital gains or multiple income sources, it may take 10-15 days. We keep you updated throughout the process.

What documents do I need for NRI tax filing?

+

You'll need Form 16/TDS certificates, bank statements, investment proofs, salary slips, property documents (if applicable), and details of foreign income. We provide a comprehensive checklist based on your specific situation.
